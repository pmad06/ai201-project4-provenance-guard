# Provence Guard - Planning 

## Detection Signals

### Signal 1: Groq LLM 
- **Measures:** whether the text reads as AI or human holistically
- **Output:** a float between 0-1 (1 = confident AI, 0 = confident human)
- **Blind spot:** formal human writing, heavily edited AI text 

### Signal 2: Stylometric Heuristics
- **Measures:** three structural properties:
  - Sentence length variance (AI text is more uniform)
  - Type-token ratio — unique words ÷ total words (AI reuses vocab predictably)
  - Punctuation density (AI uses punctuation more consistently)
- **Output:** a float between 0–1 (1 = AI-like structure, 0 = human-like structure)
- **Blind spot:** simple human writing, deliberately varied AI output

### Combining signals
- Weighted average: Signal 1 (60%) + Signal 2 (40%)
- Signal 1 gets more weight because semantic patterns are harder to fake than structure

## Uncertainty Representation

| Confidence Score | Category | Label Variant |
|------------------|----------|---------------|
|   0.70 – 1.00    | Likely AI | High-confidence AI label |
|   0.40 – 0.69    | Uncertain | Uncertain label |
|   0.00 – 0.39    | Likely Human | High-confidence human label |

Scores of 0.51 and 0.95 fall in their own categories as a score of 0.51 gets the uncertain label while a score of 0.95 gets the high-confidence AI label. 

## Transparency Label Variants

### High-confidence AI (score >= 0.70)
> "Our system has determined with high confidence that this content
> was likely AI-generated. This assessment is based on writing style
> and structural analysis. If you believe this is incorrect, you may
> submit an appeal."

### Uncertain (score 0.40 - 0.69)
> "Our system could not confidently determine whether this content
> was written by a human or generated by AI. It has been marked for
> review. If you are the creator, you may submit an appeal to provide
> additional context."

### High-confidence human (score < 0.40)
> "Our system has determined with high confidence that this content
> was likely written by a human. No further action is required."

## Appeals Workflow
- **Who can appeal:** any creator, using their content_id
- **What they provide:** content_id + written reasoning
- **What the system does:**
  1. Looks up the content_id in the log
  2. Updates status from "classified" to "under_review"
  3. Logs the appeal reasoning alongside the original decision
  4. Returns a confirmation to the creator
- **What a human reviewer sees:** the original text, both signal
  scores, the confidence score, the label that was shown, and
  the creator's reasoning

## Edge Cases

1. **Non-native English speakers:** formal, careful writing with low
   vocabulary variance will score high on stylometrics even if
   genuinely human-written. The asymmetry hint in the spec applies
   here — we should bias toward "uncertain" rather than "likely AI"
   when signals conflict.

2. **Very short text:** a haiku or a single sentence doesn't give
   stylometrics enough data to be meaningful. Sentence length
   variance is undefined with 1–2 sentences. We should flag
   submissions under ~50 words as "insufficient data" or weight
   Signal 1 more heavily.

### Narrative
A submitted text passes through two independent signals — one semantic
(Groq LLM), one structural (stylometrics). Their scores are combined
into a single confidence score via weighted average. That score selects
one of three transparency label variants, and the full decision is
written to the audit log before the response is returned.

For appeals, the creator provides their content_id and reasoning. The
system updates the record's status and appends the appeal to the log
for human review — no automated re-classification occurs.
